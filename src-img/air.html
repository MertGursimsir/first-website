AIR

Air is both hot and wet (for air is like vapor).

Air was used by Hippocrates in describing the human body and other elements too. Air was associated with blood. Also another historical figure Proclus maintained that air and each of the other elements has three properties:

FIRE Sharp Subtle Mobile
AIR Blunt Subtle Mobile
WATER Blunt Dense Mobile
EARTH Blunt Dense Immobile

Air was considered a “pure” element, but in fact the air that’s all around us is made up of a variety of gases: primarily nitrogen and oxygen, with almost 1% argon and even smaller amounts of carbon dioxide and other elements such as krypton and helium. The composition of air is just right for life on Earth, though.

by François Boucher, 1769

Air all around us actually presses down on us all the time. We would collapse under the weight of the air, except air is also inside us and exerts pressure that balances out the pressure exerted by the outside air.
